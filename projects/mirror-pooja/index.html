The Problem

Your network is enormous.
Your memory isn't.

You've met thousands of people. You've built real relationships. But most of that knowledge lives in the back of your head — fuzzy, inaccessible, and slowly fading.

🧠

The Context Problem

You meet someone at an event, exchange cards, connect on LinkedIn. Six months later you can't remember what they cared about, what you talked about, or who introduced you.

🕸️

The Network Blindspot

You have 20,000 LinkedIn connections. You have no idea most of them exist. The people most relevant to your next move are invisible inside that noise.

📂

The Scattered Data Problem

Your knowledge about people lives in Gmail, iMessage, Notes, Notion, LinkedIn, and your head. It's never connected. It never learns. It never talks back.

Architecture

Three layers. One living brain.

Mirror isn't a single app. It's a system — purpose-built to grow more intelligent every time you use it.

01
🕸️

Neo4j Graph

The structural layer. Every person, org, event, and relationship stored as nodes and edges. Query anything: "Who do I know at Techstars in Florida?"

02
📓

Obsidian Vault

The narrative layer. Rich markdown notes for every person — bios, meeting notes, context, history. Human-readable, AI-searchable.

03
📇

Google Contacts

The action layer. Synced contact profiles with enriched metadata — everywhere you already work, Mirror's knowledge shows up.

04
🤖

AI Skills Engine

Modular capabilities like people-intel (auto-research new contacts) and linkedin-cleanup (filter signal from noise) — new skills ship in a day.
