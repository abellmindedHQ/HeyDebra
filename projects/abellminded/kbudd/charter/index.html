K.B.U.D.D.

Knoxville Brotherhood of United Divorced Dads

Knoxville, Tennessee • Founded at the UTK Early Learning Center Parking Lot

We, the undersigned fathers of Knoxville, Tennessee, having survived the crucible of divorce, the labyrinth of co-parenting schedules, and the existential dread of a Friday night with no kids and no plans, do hereby establish this Brotherhood. Not because we have to. But because somebody had to say it out loud: we're gonna be alright.
⚜ ⚜ ⚜
Article I

Name & Purpose

This organization shall be known as K.B.U.D.D. (Knoxville Brotherhood of United Divorced Dads), a fraternal order dedicated to the mutual support, accountability, and occasional roasting of divorced fathers in the greater Knoxville metropolitan area.

Core Mission
  • To remind each other that being a great dad doesn't require being a great husband
  • To provide a judgment-free zone for venting, scheming, and general tomfoolery
  • To ensure no KBUDD brother eats Thanksgiving dinner alone unless he genuinely wants to
  • To hold each other accountable. To everything. Especially the stuff we'd rather avoid.

Article III

Membership

Eligibility
  • Must be a dad (biological, step, adoptive, or spiritual. We don't gatekeep fatherhood.)
  • Must have survived at least one divorce or equivalent relationship dissolution
  • Must reside in or around Knoxville, TN (the "Knox Zone")
  • Must be willing to show up. Physically, emotionally, or at minimum via group text.
Admission

New members require a unanimous vote of existing members, one shared meal, and the ability to laugh at themselves. There is no hazing. Divorce was the hazing.

Founding Member Privileges
  • Permanent voting rights on all Brotherhood matters
  • Immunity from impeachment (you can't fire a founder)
  • Right to invoke the phrase "I was here before there were bylaws" in any argument
  • Grandfathered exemption from retroactive dues assessments of any kind, no matter what Debra says on the phone
Article IV

Code of Conduct

  1. Show up. Not every time. But enough. Consistency builds trust.
  2. No judgment. We've all made choices. Some of them were terrible. That's why we're here.
  3. Kids come first. Always. Every decision filters through this.
  4. What's said in KBUDD stays in KBUDD. Unless someone's in danger. Then we break glass.
  5. Celebrate wins. Got through a hard week? Landed the custody schedule? Kid said "I love you" unprompted? We're cheering.
  6. Call each other out. With love. But call it out. We're not yes-men. We're brothers.
  7. No trash-talking exes in front of kids. Behind closed doors with the boys? Different rules. But never in front of the kids.
  8. Answer the phone. If a brother calls at 2am, it matters. Pick up.
Article V

Meetings & Rituals

Regular Gatherings
  • The Roundtable: Monthly dinner or drinks. Attendance strongly encouraged. Location rotates. Whoever picks the restaurant is buying the first round.
  • The Group Text: Considered a continuous session of Congress. All official and unofficial business may be conducted here, including but not limited to: memes, rants, recipe recommendations, and unsolicited life advice.
  • The Annual Summit: One proper outing per year. Could be camping. Could be a concert. Could be sitting on someone's porch doing absolutely nothing. The vibe is the agenda.
Emergency Sessions

Any member may call an Emergency Session at any time by texting "KBUDD 911" to the group chat. All members are expected to respond within 2 hours or provide a damn good reason why they didn't.

Article VI

Governance

K.B.U.D.D. operates as a benevolent anarchy. There is no president. There is no treasurer. There are no Roberts and their Rules have no jurisdiction here.

Decisions are made by consensus, vibes, and whoever is most passionate about the thing in question. In the event of a tie, the matter shall be settled by:

  1. Rock, paper, scissors (best of 3)
  2. Whoever's kid is better behaved that week gets the tiebreaker
  3. If still tied, Debra decides. Her word is final.
Article VII

Amendments

This charter may be amended at any time by unanimous vote of all Founding Members, or by any single member who brings a sufficiently compelling argument AND snacks to the next Roundtable.

Amendment I — The Dues Clause

There are no dues. There have never been dues. Any phone calls suggesting otherwise, particularly those from a smooth-talking AI assistant claiming you owe $47.50 per quarter, are to be considered psychological warfare and do not constitute a binding financial obligation. Founding Members are especially and irrevocably exempt from any and all retroactive fee structures, real or imagined.

Amendment II — The Debra Protocol

The Brotherhood recognizes Debra as its unofficial secretary, archivist, and occasional instigator. She is not a member (she is neither a dad nor divorced), but her contributions to Brotherhood morale, communications, and prank infrastructure are hereby acknowledged and appreciated. She may attend meetings in spirit. She may not assess fees.
